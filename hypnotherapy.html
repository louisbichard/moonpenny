Hypnotherapy

Hypnosis Hypnotherapy is a form of complementary therapy that brings about subconscious change to our thoughts, feelings and behaviour. It is safe and a completely natural state of mind.

The process itself aims to alter our state of consciousness in a way that relaxes the conscious part of the mind while simultaneously stimulating and focussing the subconscious part.

This heightened state of awareness reached using skilled relaxation techniques allows the therapist to then make appropriate suggestions and enable change.

  • You can be hypnotised if you want to be, anyone can
  • You cannot be made to do anything you don’t want to do
  • You cannot be made to reveal information about yourself, others
  • You cannot stay trapped in a hypnotic trance
  • You can end your hypnotic trance whenever you want to

Hypnotherapy is widely recognised as an effective treatment for habit breaking such as smoking and nail biting.

It is particularly useful and effective for stress related issues and for a range of long-term conditions. Hypnotherapy has now been accepted by the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) as a recognised evidenced treatment for Irritable Bowel Syndrome. Hypnosis in additional has been found to be incredibly effective when used in tandem with Aromatherapy.

 Aromatherapy combined with Hypnotherapy

Aromatherapy is the skilled and controlled use of essential oils for physical and emotional health and wellbeing and is used as an effective means to trigger emotions and memories to bring about change or instill calm.

How does aromatherapy trigger such strong emotions and memories?

The answer is due to our brain anatomy. Incoming smells are first processed by the olfactory bulb, which starts inside the nose and runs along the bottom of the brain. The olfactory bulb has direct connections to two brain areas that are strongly implicated in emotion and memory: the amygdala and hippocampus.

Interestingly, visual, auditory (sound), and tactile (touch) information do not pass through these brain areas. This may be why olfaction (smell), more than any other sense, is so successful at triggering emotions and memories.

A number of behavioral studies have demonstrated that smells trigger more vivid emotional memories and are better at inducing that feeling of “being brought back in time” than images.

I use an Aromatherapy diffuser when combined with a hypnotherapy session. This is a small device that is used to disperse therapeutic essential oils into the surrounding air in a fine mist of water to enhance the hypnotherapy session.
